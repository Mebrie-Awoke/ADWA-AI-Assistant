The Road to Adwa

A chronological breakdown of the historic events.

Treaty of Wuchale
May 1889

Dispute over Article XVII sparks the conflict. The Amharic version states options for foreign communication, while the Italian version claims absolute subjugation.

Italian Advance
1895

Italian forces cross the Mareb River into Tigray. Emperor Menelik II officially calls for mass mobilization, and forces from all over Ethiopia unite.

Battle of Adwa
Mar 1, 1896

At dawn, Ethiopian forces launch a massive coordinated attack in the mountains of Adwa, neutralizing the Italian artillery and completely destroying the invader's army.

Treaty of Addis Ababa
Oct 1896

Italy is permanently forced to sign a new treaty explicitly abolishing the Treaty of Wuchale and recognizing Ethiopia as an absolute, independent sovereign nation.

Key Figures of Adwa

The leaders who shaped history on the battlefield.

Emperor Menelik II

The Emperor of Ethiopia who expertly played European colonial powers against one another, imported modern firearms, and united rival Ethiopian provinces into a massive, unified fighting force.

Supreme Commander Strategic Genius

Empress Taytu Betul

A fierce diplomat who adamantly opposed Italian deceit. Not only a political advisor, she commanded her own battalion of 5,000 guards and architected the cutting off of the Italian water supply at Mekelle.

Master Diplomat Military Leader

Ras Alula Engida

Considered one of the greatest African generals, he organized intelligence and led the vanguard attack that completely cut off the Italian forces, sealing their doom among the mountains.

Chief of Staff Vanguard Commander

Gen. Oreste Baratieri
